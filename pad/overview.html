Robin Labs Pad — how it works, end to end

A one-transaction launch into a real Uniswap v3 pool (DexScreener from block one), bonding-curve price discovery, a strategic “let it ride” graduation, and a permanently-locked protocol-owned floor — the Bond. Creator-first economics; provably un-ruggable liquidity.

Contracts LIVE on mainnet Internal audit: 3 passes ✓ External audit: pending

00What it is

A creator deploys a coin in a single call. It goes live instantly as a genuine Uniswap v3 pool — chartable, tradeable, indexable by any aggregator from the first block. Buyers walk the price up a single-sided “curve.” When the coin is successful enough, it graduates: the raised ETH is locked into a protocol-owned market maker (the Bond) that stands a floor under the price forever. The creator earns from trading and a graduation reward; the platform earns a slim swap fee; nobody can pull the liquidity.

The whole system is seven small contracts. No proxies, no upgradeability, no admin mint, no pause. What ships is what runs.

02The full flow — ① Launch

One transaction. The dev calls factory.launch(name, symbol, tax), optionally sending ETH for their own opening buy.

Deploy the token

A clean ERC-20 via CREATE2 with per-launch entropy — the address (and therefore the pool) can’t be predicted from a nonce, so nobody can pre-init the pool to brick the launch.

Create + initialize the pool

A real Uniswap v3 pool (1% tier) is created and initialized at the start price (~$4k FDV). DexScreener + aggregators see it now

Seed the curve

75% of supply is minted as a single-sided token position spanning [start → ceiling]. That position is the bonding curve — buyers swap ETH in and walk the price up it. The other 25% is held back as the Ambush reserve.

Open trading + arm the guard

Trading latches on and the anti-snipe window starts (buy-side caps, cooldown, dead window). The pool’s TWAP oracle is armed for the Bond’s later guard.

Register fees + optional dev buy

The coin’s fee config registers with the router (once, immutable). If the dev sent ETH, an atomic ≤2% buy executes now — ahead of the field, sniper-protected.

ResultA live, tradeable, charted coin with ~1B supply, a ~$4k starting FDV, and a curve that graduates somewhere between $30k and $76k. Zero cost to the platform — the token seeds its own liquidity; buyers bring the ETH.

03② Trade — the curve phase

Every trade goes through PadRouter. Buys take native ETH (no approval); sells need one exact-amount token approval. Price walks up the single-sided curve as ETH comes in.

  • Anti-snipe window (opening ~5 min): buys face a dead window, per-wallet max-tx / max-wallet caps that loosen in phases, a cooldown, and an optional known-bot blocklist. Sells are never restricted — this can’t become a honeypot.
  • After the window the token is a permanently normal ERC-20; the guard is a one-SLOAD short-circuit.
  • Fees are applied per trade (see Economics) and held as escrow — never a side-transfer inside the user’s trade, so a trade can’t revert on a fee payout.

04③ Graduate — “let it ride”

Graduation isn’t a fixed line — it’s a strategic window. It becomes eligible at the minimum (~$30k mcap / ~4 ETH raised) and can happen anywhere up to the ceiling (~$76k / ~8.3 ETH). The further the coin rides before graduating, the bigger the raise and the thicker the floor.

ConceptWhat it isWho controls it
minGradTickThe earliest price graduation is allowed (~$30k).Fixed at launch
gradTargetThe auto-graduate price. Default sits 40% up the range (~$45k). A keeper/frontend fires the permissionless graduate() once price reaches it.Dev (setGradTarget), within [min, ceiling]
gradTick (ceiling)The curve’s top (~$76k). Buys can’t push past it; graduation can’t post above it.Fixed at launch
Timeout fallbackAfter 7 days, anyone may graduate at the minimum even if the dev set a high target and walked away.Permissionless
Why it mattersA sniper can’t graduate a coin early to lock a thin floor against the dev’s wishes — graduate() stays locked until the dev’s target (or the 7-day fallback). But the floor can be delayed, never denied.

When graduate() fires it: collects the raised WETH + unsold curve tokens → pays the creator 25% of the raise → posts the Bond with the rest: 60% into the Sherwood LP, 40% into the Bounty floor, and the 25% reserve + all unsold curve tokens into the Ambush wall.

05④ The Bond — the locked floor, forever

A protocol-owned market maker, posted once and locked. It holds three Uniswap v3 positions around the price:

▲ higher price
Ambush
Token sell-wall, ~3×–25× above price. Sells only into strength; the WETH it earns flows into the Bounty floor. Holds the 25% reserve + rolled-in unsold curve tokens.
Sherwood
Full-range locked LP (~60% of the raise). Principal is never withdrawn — and its swap fees compound straight back into the position on every poke, so the permanent liquidity only ever grows.
Bounty
WETH buy-wall just below price down to ~-49% (~40% of the raise). This is the floor — a ladder of bids that catches dips.
▼ lower price

A permissionless poke() recenters the Bounty (all held WETH) and Ambush (all held tokens) around the current price — ratcheting the floor up after a pump and recycling caught supply after a dump — guarded by a spot-vs-TWAP check so it can’t be poked at a manipulated price.

Anti-rug by constructionThere is no function that sends the Bond’s WETH or tokens to any address. Sherwood principal never leaves; Bounty/Ambush funds only ever become pool positions. No owner, no setter, no drain.

06Economics — who earns what

Every coin runs a swap fee of 1%–4% per side (1% floor, 4% cap, set by the dev at launch). The default 1% base is split by side:

FlowGoes toDetail
Buy 1% basePlatform0.9% immediately + 0.1% released at graduation
Sell 1% baseCreatoraccrues to the project wallet — the creator’s ongoing income
Above-1% excess → 25%Platformthe buy-back cut (platform buys/burns its token off-chain)
Above-1% excess → 75%Projectdev-chosen split across wallet / Bond floor / auto-burn
Graduation rewardCreator25% of the raise (~1 ETH) paid at graduation — the launch incentive
Uniswap LP feesThe coincompound into the locked Sherwood LP forever — the floor deepens with volume

Creator income = the 1% sell tax + the 25% graduation reward + their wallet share of any raised tax. Platform income = the 1% buy tax + the 25% cut on raised tax. Rounding is exact to the wei; every share is escrow, paid out by separate permissionless flushers so a trade can never revert on a payout.

08Anti-rug & security posture

  • Clean token — no mint, no fee-on-transfer, no pause, no owner switch, no blacklist over sells. After the anti-snipe window it’s an ordinary ERC-20 that reads clean on every honeypot scanner.
  • Locked liquidity — the Bond never sends WETH or tokens to any wallet; Sherwood principal is never withdrawn. Liquidity can only grow.
  • Floor-drain closed — graduation refuses to post above the ceiling (the only unbacked price zone); anywhere inside the curve, moving price up costs real WETH that joins the raise, so a manipulated graduation price is one the attacker paid for, and the floor sits below it. Proven un-drainable by the audit fleet.
  • Conservation — every wei of WETH and every token is accounted for across the whole lifecycle; verified to the wei by a 300-run fuzz (1,592 ops) and the graduation battery.
  • Ownership — Ownable2Step; renounceOwnership is disabled (payouts route to the owner, so ownership is load-bearing).
  • No proxies / no upgradeability — the deployed bytecode is final.

09Creator & keeper controls

Creator only

setGradTarget

Move the auto-graduate price anywhere in [min, ceiling] — lower to graduate sooner, higher to ride for a thicker floor.

Creator only

burnDev

Instead of collecting sell fees, spend them buying the coin and burning it. Only the creator can torch their own escrow.

Public

withdrawDev

Anyone can trigger it; the creator’s accrued sell fees always pay to the project wallet.

Public keeper

graduate()

Fires once price reaches the target (or after the 7-day timeout). A frontend/bot calls it — the “graduate” button.

Public keeper

poke()

Recenters the Bond’s floor and sell-wall; TWAP-guarded. Anyone can call.

Public

flush / claim / withdraw

Permissionless payout of each escrow bucket to its fixed destination (platform, project wallet, floor, burn).

11Audit status

Three internal passes are complete: a manual review, a deep pre-production audit, and a third adversarial pass — a 5-auditor fleet over every attack surface plus a 300-run fuzz and a random-graduation battery. The core was attacked hard and held: floor-drain proven impossible, conservation exact, floor monotonic, anti-rug intact. One real fee-evasion bug was found and fixed; defensive guards and two economic-safety hardenings were added.

The full lifecycle was then validated live on mainnet (launch → anti-snipe → buy → graduate → Bond posts all three positions + pays the creator reward). Findings and resolutions are in launchpad/AUDIT.md; the code is in launchpad/contracts/. This document is the orientation for the external audit now underway.
